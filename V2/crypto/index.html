Step-by-Step Flow

Three phases: Taiwan → MetaMask → Spend or SGD

Phase A: Taiwan On-Ramp 🇹🇼

1. Choose an FSC-registered exchange

Recommended: MAX Exchange (MaiCoin) or BitoPro. Both are registered with Taiwan's Financial Supervisory Commission since Nov 2024.

💡 Why FSC-registered matters: Your funds are held in segregated trust accounts. If the exchange fails, your money is protected.
2. Register and complete KYC

Upload your Taiwan National ID, verify phone and email, and enable 2FA. Approval typically takes 1–3 business days.

ID Upload Phone Verify 2FA Setup
3. Deposit TWD from Bank of Taiwan

Link your Bank of Taiwan account and initiate a transfer to the exchange's trust account. Deposits are usually same-day during banking hours.

⚡ Tip: Transfer early in the day (before 3 PM) for same-day crediting.
4. Buy cryptocurrency

Most users buy USDT (stablecoin) first, then swap if needed. Fees: ~0.2% trading fee + 0.1%–0.3% spread on MAX/BitoPro.

Phase B: Transfer to MetaMask 🦊

5. Set up MetaMask

Install the browser extension or mobile app. Create a wallet and save your seed phrase offline on paper. Never store it digitally.

🚨 Critical: Anyone with your seed phrase can steal all your crypto. Treat it like your bank PIN.
6. Withdraw from exchange to MetaMask

In the exchange, go to Withdraw → select your crypto. Choose the network carefully — this affects speed and cost:

Legal & Tax Considerations

  • • Not legal tender: Crypto is not recognized as currency in Taiwan
  • • FSC registration required: All VASPs must register since Nov 2024. Only use FSC-registered exchanges.
  • • Credit card ban: You cannot buy crypto with credit cards in Taiwan
  • • No specific crypto tax yet: But keep detailed records — a crypto tax law may come in 2025–2026
  • • Keep records: All transaction dates, amounts, fees, and exchange rates for potential future tax filing
  • • No capital gains tax for individuals: Major advantage! Personal crypto gains are not taxed.
  • • Income tax applies if trading as a business: 0%–24% progressive rate if MAS determines you're operating a trading business
  • • MAS licensing required: Use only MAS-licensed exchanges (DigiToken, Crypto.com, OKX Singapore)
  • • FATF Travel Rule: Applies to transfers above ~SGD 1,500 — exchanges may require additional info
  • • Record keeping: Maintain records for 5 years in case of audit
  • • Taiwan to Singapore crypto transfer: Not a taxable event in either jurisdiction (for individuals)
  • • Currency conversion: TWD → Crypto → SGD creates exchange rate exposure
  • • Reporting: No mandatory cross-border reporting for amounts under investigation thresholds
  • • Documentation: Keep exchange records, wallet addresses, and transaction hashes for all transfers
  • • Disclaimer: Tax laws change. Consult a qualified tax advisor for your specific situation.
